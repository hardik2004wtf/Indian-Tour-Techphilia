Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, an iconic symbol of love and architectural brilliance, stands proudly on the banks of the Yamuna River in Agra, India.

By the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal.

This magnificent white marble mausoleum is considered one of the world's most stunning wonders.

Built between 1632 and 1653.

The result is the Taj Mahal, a symbol of enduring love and a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1983.

Golden Temple, Gadisar Lake & Akshardham

A radiant spiritual and cultural jewel located in the heart of Amritsar, Punjab, India. Constructed in the 16th century by Guru Arjan Dev, the fifth Sikh Guru, the Golden Temple is a harmonious blend of Islamic and Hindu architectural styles.

Gadisar Lake, a picturesque man-made reservoir in the heart of Jaisalmer, Rajasthan, India, stands as a tranquil oasis amidst the arid landscape of the Thar Desert. Constructed in the 14th century by Maharaja Gadsi Sing

Akshardham, a magnificent temple complex located in Delhi, India.

Officially known as the Swaminarayan Akshardham Temple.

The temple is dedicated to Bhagwan Swaminarayan and showcases the traditional art and cultural values of India.
